History

Old Harbour, town and Caribbean port, southern Jamaica, west of Kingston. Originally called Esquivel after its Spanish founder, it was once Spanish Town’s second port, when the latter was capital of Jamaica, and was famous for shipbuilding. After a period of decline it is now important for shipping bauxite as well as sugarcane, tropical fruit, and coffee. In 1940 the U.S. Navy leased land on nearby Little Goat Island and established a base there that was used during World War II. Pop. (2011) urban area, 28,912..

The Old harbour Clock However, in 1892 the St Catherine Parochial Board, more refer today as the Parish Council among locals but officially as the municipal corporation, agreed that a town clock be placed in Old Harbour Square. This motion was moved “on March 10, 1892” by “Granville Goldsboro (sometimes spelt Goldsburgh)”.

However, consent had to be first sought from the Governor of the parish, who in today’s day and age is most likely the mayor and chairman of the St Catherine Municipal Corporation. This was done and included in this letter of request was the approval and release £5 for the budget to erect the clock in Old Harbour.
